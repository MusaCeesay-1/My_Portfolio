Healthcare Appointment Scheduling & Intake Optimization

A second analyst case demonstrating repeatable delivery in a different domain. This solution redesigns outpatient scheduling and intake to reduce no-shows, shorten check-in time, and improve provider utilization.

Domain: Healthcare Operations

Role: Business Systems Analyst

Status: Analysis Package Complete

Problem, Goals, and Metrics

Problem Statement

Patients experience long call wait times, duplicate data entry at check-in, and poor appointment visibility. Front desk teams manually reconcile scheduling, EHR records, and reminder logs, creating no-shows and delays.

Business Goals

  1. Reduce no-show rate from 18% to 10%.
  2. Cut average check-in time from 14 minutes to 6 minutes.
  3. Increase same-week appointment fill rate by 25%.
  4. Reduce duplicate patient-intake data entry by 90%.
  5. Improve patient satisfaction for scheduling and arrival experience.

Success Metrics (KPIs)

  • No-show percentage
  • Average check-in duration
  • Appointment slot utilization
  • Reminder confirmation rate
  • Patient satisfaction score

AS-IS and TO-BE Process Design

AS-IS

  1. Patient calls front desk for scheduling.
  2. Staff enters appointment in scheduler and separately updates EHR notes.
  3. Reminder list is exported manually at day end.
  4. Patient repeats demographics and insurance at check-in.
  5. Eligibility issues discovered late, causing visit delays.

TO-BE

  1. Patient books online or by phone through one scheduling workflow.
  2. System validates demographics and insurance in advance.
  3. Automated reminders collect confirmation or reschedule response.
  4. Digital pre-check-in populates EHR intake forms.
  5. Front desk handles exception-only cases with full context.

Requirements, Stories, and Acceptance Criteria

Functional Requirements

  • Unified scheduling workflow across phone and portal channels.
  • Real-time eligibility and insurance validation before visit date.
  • Automated reminders with confirm/reschedule capability.
  • Digital pre-check-in synced to EHR intake fields.
  • Exception queue for unresolved eligibility or incomplete forms.

User Stories + Acceptance Criteria

US-01: Patient Self-Scheduling

Story: As a patient, I want to book or reschedule online so I can avoid phone delays.

Acceptance: Given open slots exist, when I select a slot, then confirmation is sent within 30 seconds.

US-02: Front Desk Visibility

Story: As front desk staff, I want readiness indicators so I can prioritize arrivals with unresolved issues.

Acceptance: Given missing pre-check-in data, when the patient arrives, then the queue marks the visit as "Action Required".

US-03: Provider Utilization

Story: As a clinic manager, I want no-show predictions so I can backfill likely open slots.

Acceptance: Given high no-show risk, when 24-hour reminder runs, then standby waitlist offers are triggered.

Assumptions, Risks, and Scope

Assumptions

  • EHR supports API-based intake updates.
  • Patients can receive SMS and email reminders.
  • Clinic policies permit digital pre-check-in workflows.

Risks

  • Legacy EHR integration constraints.
  • Patient adoption variability by demographic segment.
  • Insurer API outages impacting pre-visit checks.

Scope Boundaries

In Scope: Outpatient scheduling, reminders, pre-check-in, front desk exceptions.

Out Of Scope: Inpatient bed management, clinical treatment workflows, claims adjudication.
